The Practice

What is
Reflexology?

Reflexology is a compression technique applied to specific points — called reflexes — on your feet or hands. These points correspond to all organs and parts of the body, and when stimulated, work to improve circulation and normalize the body's natural rhythms.

It is a deeply relaxing, non-invasive treatment that supports the whole body — not just a single symptom.

A Practice as Old as Humanity

The modern practice of reflexology has its origins in the 19th century and was fully developed in the 1930s. Yet archaeological evidence suggests that applying pressure to the hands and feet is an ancient tradition. In several cultures, the foot is considered a conduit to spirituality and general wellness.
